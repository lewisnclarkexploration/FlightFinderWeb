Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Introduction

FlightFinder is an aircraft tracking application that uses publicly available ADS-B data via the OpenSky Network to display aircraft near your location. This privacy policy explains what information is collected and how it is handled.

In short: FlightFinder collects no personal data, uses no analytics SDKs, and displays no advertisements. The only data that leaves your device is your approximate location, sent solely to query the OpenSky Network for nearby aircraft.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Approximate Location

FlightFinder uses your device's approximate location (coarse city-level accuracy) to query the OpenSky Network API for aircraft in your vicinity.

  • Your approximate location is sent to OpenSky Network solely to retrieve publicly available aircraft transponder data in your area.
  • It is not stored on your device beyond the current session.
  • It is not shared with any other third party.
  • It is not associated with any personal identifier such as an account or device ID.

You can verify this in the app's source code, as FlightFinder is built with transparency in mind.

1.2 Camera Access

FlightFinder may request camera access for the optional augmented reality (AR) view. When enabled:

  • The camera feed is processed entirely on your device.
  • No images, video, or data from the camera are ever transmitted, stored, or shared.
  • Camera access can be revoked at any time in your device settings.

1.3 Sensor Data

FlightFinder uses your device's gyroscope, accelerometer, and magnetometer to determine device orientation for the sky pointer feature.

  • All sensor data is processed locally on your device.
  • It is never transmitted or stored.

1.4 What We Do NOT Collect

FlightFinder does not collect, store, or transmit:

  • Names, email addresses, or any personally identifiable information.
  • Account credentials or login information.
  • Device identifiers, IMEI, or advertising IDs.
  • Payment information.
  • Browsing history, crash logs, or usage analytics.

2. Third-Party Services

2.1 OpenSky Network

When you use FlightFinder, your approximate location is sent to OpenSky Network to retrieve publicly available aircraft transponder (ADS-B) data in your area. OpenSky Network is a non-profit association providing open access to ADS-B data for research and non-commercial use.

The information received from OpenSky (aircraft positions, callsigns, velocities) is publicly available and does not contain user data. OpenSky Network's own privacy policy applies to any data they process.

2.2 ADSBDB

FlightFinder queries the ADSBDB API using public aircraft ICAO24 transponder codes to enrich flight information (route, airline). No user data is transmitted.

2.3 Airport-data.com

FlightFinder queries airport-data.com for aircraft photographs using public aircraft registration numbers. No user data is transmitted.

2.4 Flagsapi.com

FlightFinder uses flagsapi.com to display country flags based on ISO country codes derived from the aircraft's route. No user data is transmitted.

3. Data Storage

FlightFinder stores only user preferences locally on your device using Android's EncryptedSharedPreferences (AES-256 encryption). These preferences include:

  • Display preferences (show altitude, airline logos, contrail trail)
  • Measurement units (meters/feet, kilometers/miles, km/h/mph)
  • Language selection
  • Field of view settings
  • Search radius
  • Number of flights viewed (for rating prompt)

No flight data, location data, or personal data is stored persistently. All preferences are deleted when the app is uninstalled.

4. Children's Privacy

FlightFinder does not knowingly collect any personal information from children under the age of 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe that your child has provided personal data, please contact us so that we can investigate and remove such data.

5. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected on this page with an updated revision date. We encourage you to review this page periodically.
